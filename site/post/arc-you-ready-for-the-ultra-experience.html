Arc You Ready for the Ultra Experience? Sonos Unveils Arc Ultra Soundbar and Sub 4 with Immersive Spatial Audio

Sonos has officially taken the wraps off its latest, the Arc Ultra soundbar, alongside a new and improved Sub 4 wireless subwoofer.

In a reveal following weeks of leaks, Sonos aims to push the boundaries of home theater audio with a 9.1.4-channel setup, Spatial Audio support, and advanced sound-enhancing tech. Here’s everything you need to know about these premium sound upgrades coming to a living room near you.

‍

‍

Arc Ultra Soundbar: Redefining Immersive Audio

The Sonos Arc Ultra is a step up from the beloved Sonos Arc, boasting a hefty $999 price tag but packing a punch with premium features. With a revamped 9.1.4-channel system and Spatial Audio technology, Sonos is upping the ante on immersive soundscapes, allowing users to experience every layer of audio from all directions. At the heart of this audio beast is Sonos’s new Sound Motion transducer, a feature that may have been inspired by Mayht, a startup Sonos acquired in 2022. According to Sonos, this new transducer provides twice the bass depth, enhancing the listening experience with richer, more resonant lows.

Inside, the Arc Ultra packs serious specs, including 2GB of RAM, 8GB of memory, and an array of 15 Class-D amplifiers, all fine-tuned to elevate audio clarity. Additionally, it features a unique internal woofer system paired with waveguides and tweeters, creating a balanced audio field that complements the Spatial Audio experience. It’s also equipped with Sonos’s TruePlay tuning feature, allowing users to adjust sound quality based on the room’s acoustics, ensuring that every note and beat is as vibrant as intended.

Sonos has included several speech enhancement features, building on its successes with the Era series. The Arc Ultra’s enhanced center channel architecture and refined speech enhancement functions promise crystal-clear dialogue, perfect for action-packed films and dialogue-heavy TV shows.

‍

‍

A Familiar, Sleek Design

In terms of aesthetics, the Arc Ultra follows in the footsteps of its predecessor, with a sleek, curved design that fits Sonos’s signature minimalist aesthetic. It comes in both black and white finishes, with a long, rounded front panel and a smooth matte look that easily blends into any modern home.

‍

‍

Sonos Sub 4: The Low-End Upgrade

Joining the Arc Ultra is the Sonos Sub 4, a next-generation subwoofer designed to bring even deeper bass to the Sonos ecosystem. Priced at $799, the Sub 4 isn’t exactly an entry-level choice, but it offers premium features for serious audio enthusiasts. With two inward-facing 8-inch woofers and advanced WiFi radios, the Sub 4 is built to provide both power and connectivity, reducing idle power consumption by 50% compared to previous models.

Unlike the Sonos Sub Mini, the Sub 4 can pair with multiple Sonos devices simultaneously, making it compatible with the entire Sonos lineup, including the Ray, Beam, Era 100, and Era 300 speakers. This flexibility makes the Sub 4 an attractive choice for users looking to expand their Sonos sound system across different rooms or create a dynamic, layered audio experience.

‍

‍

Preorders and Availability

Both the Arc Ultra soundbar and the Sub 4 subwoofer are available for preorder on Sonos’s website and Amazon, with an official release date set for October 29. Whether you’re looking to upgrade your home theater setup or want to bring concert-quality sound into your living room, Sonos’s latest offerings deliver top-tier performance at a premium price.

The Arc Ultra and Sub 4 promise to elevate audio immersion with state-of-the-art technology, sleek designs, and a commitment to redefining the home audio experience.
